A city rich in aviation history welcomes the next generation of air and spacecraft - It's time to meet the next Frontiers in Flight

From its earliest days, Kansas has been known for it's frontier towns and beautiful waving plains. The city of Wichita became known for cattle drives and trading outposts along the Chisholm Trail, eventually earning the moniker of "Cowtown." But, by the early 20th century, the forefathers of aviation saw another future for this thriving frontier city.

Starting in the 1920s and 30s, businessmen and aeronautical engineers began moving aircraft manufacturing companies to the city of Wichita. The city was soon booming with the world's top aviation companies, and the "Air Capital of the World" was born.

Wichita remains a thriving hub in the world of aviation, and this former frontier city is now on the frontier of defense technology. Come to the Frontiers in Flight Air Show and see the KC-46 Pegasus, McConnell Air Force Base's new addition to its fleet of refueling aircraft. Then, witness aviation acts from around the world, including the Air Force's most elite flyers, the USAF Thunderbirds.

From frontier days to aviation beginnings, to cutting-edge refuelers and the Air Force's continued research into space technology, the citizens of Wichita are truly a part of the Frontiers in Flight.
